Verum Wallet Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 1, 2026

1. Introduction

Verum Crypto and its affiliates ("Verum," "we," "our," or "ours") respect your privacy and are strictly committed to protecting your personal data. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, store, and use the information generated when you interact with the Verum Wallet application, our website, and related services.

As a self-custody software native to the Solana blockchain, we do not have access to, collect, or store your private keys (Seed Phrase), spending passwords, or the assets held in your wallet. You have 100% control over your assets.

By using our services, you agree to the practices described in this policy. If you do not agree, please do not use our application.

2. Who We Are and Contact Us

Data Controller: Verum Crypto, headquartered in Brazil.

Privacy Contact: For questions, requests, or to exercise your legal rights regarding your data, please contact us via email: contato@verumcrypto.com.

Third-Party Links and Integrations: Our interface includes connectivity with third-party providers for local currency/crypto conversions, and liquidity aggregators for swaps. Clicking on these links or using these widgets means interacting with independent platforms. We do not control these services and are not responsible for their policies. We strongly recommend reading the Privacy Policies of these partners.

3. The Data We Collect

Our principle is minimal data collection. However, for the application to function properly, we may process the following categories:

Information you provide to us: When you contact our support team, report a bug, or participate in community surveys, we may collect your name, email address, and the details of your message.

Technical and Usage Data: Whenever you use Verum Wallet, we may automatically collect system information. This includes IP address (anonymized when possible), device type, operating system, and interaction metrics (which screens were accessed, clicks, and errors in the app). This is vital for keeping the application fast and secure.

Blockchain Data (Public): The Solana blockchain is a public and immutable ledger. Your wallet address (public key) and the transactions made on it are visible to anyone in the world. We do not link your public wallet address to your real identity (name, CPF, etc.) unless you send us this information directly in a support request.

Financial and KYC Data (Not collected by us): As we are only the software interface, we do not perform credit card processing, PIX, or KYC (Know Your Customer) processes. When you decide to buy or sell cryptocurrencies, you will provide this data directly to our licensed partners.

4. How We Use Your Data

We use the limited data we possess exclusively to:

  • Ensure the operation, technical security, and stability of Verum Wallet.
  • Analyze usage trends to improve the user experience (UX) and payment flow in partner merchants.
  • Provide efficient technical support.
  • Prevent malicious activities, fraud, and protect the integrity of our software.

5. Data Sharing

Verum will never sell your personal data. We only share strictly necessary information in the following scenarios:

  • Infrastructure Providers: Companies that provide us with cloud hosting services, data analytics, and customer support tools, operating under strict confidentiality agreements.
  • Legal Obligations: We may share limited information we possess if required by law, court order, or applicable legal process in our jurisdiction of operation.

6. User Security and Responsibility

The security of your assets is based on the advanced cryptography of the Solana network and the biometric capabilities of your device (Face ID/Touch ID).

As Verum is a non-custodial wallet, the responsibility for storing your Recovery Phrase (12 to 24 words) in a secure, offline location rests solely with you. If you lose your Recovery Phrase or give it to scammers, Verum has no power, authority, or technical means to recover your funds, as your assets are on the blockchain and not on our servers.

7. Your Rights and Retention

We will retain your data (such as technical support logs) only for as long as necessary to fulfill the original purpose of collection. Depending on your jurisdiction, you have the right to:

  • Request access to the personal data we hold about you.
  • Request the correction of inaccurate data.
  • Request the deletion of your communication/support data.

8. Changes to this Policy

We reserve the right to update this Privacy Policy periodically to reflect technological or regulatory changes. We will notify users of significant changes through notices within the Verum Wallet application itself.
